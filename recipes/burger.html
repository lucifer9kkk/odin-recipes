The Perfect Basic Burger

Home

The perfect basic burger is a masterclass in simplicity, focusing on a juicy, high-fat ground beef patty that is seared to develop a salty, savory crust. It is served on a soft, butter-toasted bun that holds up against the moisture of melted American cheese and a variety of crisp, cool toppings like lettuce, tomato, and onion. This iconic dish relies on the contrast between the hot, tender meat and the fresh, tangy crunch of pickles and classic condiments for a balanced, satisfying bite.

Ingredients

  • Ground beef
  • Hamburger buns (brioche or sesame seed)
  • Kosher salt and black pepper
  • American cheese or cheddar slices
  • Butter (for toasting buns)
  • Vegetable oil or butter (for the pan)
  • Lettuce (iceberg or romaine)
  • Tomato slices
  • Red onion or white onion (thinly sliced)
  • Pickle chips
  • Condiments (ketchup, mustard, or mayonnaise)

Steps

  1. Divide the ground beef into equal portions without overworking the meat.
  2. Shape the portions into patties slightly wider than your hamburger buns.
  3. Press a small indentation into the center of each patty with your thumb.
  4. Season both sides of the patties generously with salt and pepper.
  5. Heat a cast-iron skillet or grill to medium-high heat with a little oil.
  6. Toast the buttered buns on the hot surface until they are golden brown.
  7. Sear the patties for 3–4 minutes until a deep brown crust forms.
  8. Flip the burgers and immediately place a cheese slice on each patty.
  9. Cook for another 3 minutes or until the cheese is fully melted.
  10. Assemble by placing the patty on the bottom bun and adding your toppings.
