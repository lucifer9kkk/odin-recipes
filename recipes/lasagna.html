Lasagna

Home

Lasagna is a classic Italian baked dish consisting of stacked, wide flat pasta sheets alternating with ingredients like ragù (meat sauce), béchamel, vegetables, and cheeses (ricotta, mozzarella, Parmesan). Originating in the Middle Ages, this casserole-style dish is typically baked until bubbly and golden, offering a rich blend of savory flavors.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Ground beef
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Crushed tomatoes or Marinara sauce
  • Tomato paste
  • Onion and Garlic
  • Egg
  • Fresh parsley, dried basil, and oregano
  • Salt and Black pepper

Steps

  1. Brown the ground beef and Italian sausage with diced onions and garlic.
  2. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, and seasonings, then simmer the sauce.
  3. Mix the ricotta cheese, egg, grated Parmesan, and fresh parsley in a bowl.
  4. Boil the lasagna noodles in salted water until al dente and drain.
  5. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a 9x13 baking dish.
  6. Layer noodles, ricotta mixture, meat sauce, and shredded mozzarella.
  7. Repeat the layers until the dish is full, finishing with a thick coating of mozzarella.
  8. Cover with foil and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25 minutes.
  9. Remove the foil and bake for another 20 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and brown.
  10. Rest the lasagna for at least 15 minutes before slicing and serving.
